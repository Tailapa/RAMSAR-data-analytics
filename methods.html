This analysis uses a combination of spatial statistics, Ramsar-criteria scoring, and text-based threat coding to evaluate India’s Ramsar network through a data-driven lens.

Ecological Diversity Score

  • For each site, the Ramsar criteria (C1–C9) listed in the Ramsar Information Sheet were encoded as binary variables (1 = criterion met, 0 = not met).
  • The diversity score equals the sum of all satisfied criteria for that site, ranging from 1 to 9.
  • Sites with scores ≥7 are treated as Irreplaceable Hubs (Bio-Ecocitadels), while scores of 1–2 indicate single-axis or minimalist profiles.

Threat Coding and Threat Density

  • Threats were extracted from the RIS “Threats” and “Factors adversely affecting the site’s ecological character” fields.
  • Individual descriptions were mapped to a standard list of anthropogenic and climatic categories (e.g., “urban development”, “agriculture”, “water regulation”, “invasive species”, “pollution”, “tourism and recreation”).
  • For each site, threat density is the count of distinct threat categories present.

Distance to Nearest Neighbour and Clustering

  • Site centroids were derived from official coordinates and projected into an equal-area coordinate system suitable for India.
  • Pairwise great-circle distances were calculated; for each wetland, the nearest-neighbour distance is the smallest of these values.
  • Sites with distances below ~10 km are interpreted as clusters/complexes, while those above ~250–300 km are treated as ecological islands.

North–South Split

  • The Tropic of Cancer (23.5°N) was used as the dividing line.
  • Sites with latitude ≥23.5°N are classified as North; those with latitude <23.5°N are classified as South.
  • Regional metrics (total, mean, and median area) were computed to identify how "mega-sites" skew national averages.

Red List Thresholds

To identify crisis sites where ecological value and pressure coincide, a Ramsar Red List was defined using two conditions:

  • High diversity: Diversity score ≥5.
  • High threat density: ≥20 distinct threats recorded for the site.
  • Sites meeting both are labeled Red List Wetlands and are prioritized for interventions.
